Astronomical Achievements!

Nancy Grace Roman

Dr. Roman was the first chief of astronomy at NASA and the first woman to hold an executive position there. She was instrumental in making the Hubble Space Telescope a reality.

Henrietta Swan Leavitt

Leavitt is best known for discovering ~2,400 variable stars in her lifetime. She discovered that some of these stars have a consistent brightness no matter where they are located, making these so-called Cepheid variables a good measuring stick for astronomical distances.

Sandra Faber

Sandra Faber is an astronomer whose research has revealed how galaxies form and evolve. She has made important discoveries linking the brightness of galaxies to the speed of stars within them and was the co-discoverer of the Faber–Jackson relation.

Famous Firsts!

Valentina Tereshkova

At just 26, Tereshkova had become the first woman to fly in space. During her three-day mission, she orbited the Earth 48 times. Her spaceflight was broadcast on television and watched by citizens in the Soviet Union and Europe.

Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was an American astronaut and engineer, who first flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a mission specialist and primary robotic arm operator.

Mae Jemison

Jemison was an American astronaut and physician who and the first African American woman to be admitted into NASA’s astronaut training program. On September 12, 1992, Jemison finally flew into space abroad Endeavour on mission STS47, becoming the first African American woman in space.
